Konidela Ram Charan

Indian Actor

Career Highlights

  • Ram Charan made his acting debut in 2007 with the film Chirutha, which earned him the Filmfare Award for Best Male Debut – South.
  • His real breakthrough came with Magadheera (2009), directed by S.S. Rajamouli.
  • This film, based on reincarnation, was a huge box office success and established him as a superstar.
  • He continued to deliver hits like Racha, Naayak, Yevadu, Govindudu Andarivadele, and Dhruva.
  • His performance in Rangasthalam (2018), where he played a partially deaf villager, won him immense critical appreciation and awards.

Personal Life

  • Ram Charan’s personal life is often admired as much as his professional achievements. Despite being one of India’s biggest stars, he is known for his grounded nature, humility, and strong family values.
  • He grew up closely attached to his parents, superstar Chiranjeevi and Surekha, and has often mentioned how his parents’ disciplined lifestyle shaped his own.
  • Being from a large film family, he shares a warm bond with his cousins like Allu Arjun and says family get-togethers are always lively and filled with laughter.
  • Ram Charan fell in love with Upasana Kamineni, who comes from a prominent business and medical family. Upasana is the granddaughter of Dr. Prathap C. Reddy, founder of Apollo Hospitals, one of Asia’s largest healthcare.
  • She is the Vice Chairperson of Apollo Foundation and works actively in healthcare philanthropy. They got engaged in December 2011 after a long-standing friendship and married on 14 June 2012 in a grand ceremony in Hyderabad attended by celebrities, politicians, and thousands of fans.
  • Their relationship is often celebrated in the media as a perfect blend of glamour and deep understanding. Ram Charan frequently praises Upasana for being a strong, independent woman who manages her own business responsibilities while supporting him wholeheartedly.
  • Upasana has said in interviews that Ram Charan is a very caring husband who believes in simple joys and enjoys spending quiet time at home despite his stardom.

Awards and Recognition

  • Ram Charan’s career has been decorated with numerous awards and honours that highlight both his talent and his popularity. Right from his debut, he started winning accolades. For his first film Chirutha (2007), he won the Filmfare Award for Best Male Debut – South, which instantly established him as one of the most promising new faces in Telugu cinema.
  • His performance in Magadheera (2009), a fantasy epic directed by S.S. Rajamouli, earned him massive critical and commercial success. For this film, he won the Filmfare Award for Best Actor – Telugu, the Nandi Special Jury Award, and the CineMAA Award for Best Actor. Magadheera ran for over 700 days in some theatres and remains one of the highest-grossing Telugu films of all time, cementing his superstar status.
  • Ram Charan has also received recognition for his versatility. His portrayal of a rugged, partially hearing-impaired village youth in Rangasthalam (2018) is widely considered his career-best performance. For this role, he won his second Filmfare Award for Best Actor – Telugu, another SIIMA Award, and many critics awards, proving that he’s more than just a commercial hero.

Education

  • Ram Charan’s educational journey reflects the typical upbringing of someone from a well-known, culturally rich South Indian family, balanced with discipline and diverse interests. Though born in Chennai (then Madras), he spent much of his early childhood moving between Chennai and Hyderabad, depending on his father Chiranjeevi’s shooting schedules.
  • He began his schooling at Padma Seshadri Bala Bhavan (PSBB) School in Chennai, one of the city’s most respected institutions, known for emphasizing both academics and extracurricular activities. Later, after the family moved to Hyderabad, he continued his studies at Lawrence School, Lovedale in Ooty— a prestigious boarding school in the Nilgiri Hills. This experience exposed him to a multi-cultural environment, sports, and team activities that built his leadership qualities and confidence.
  • Ram Charan also attended The Hyderabad Public School (HPS), Begumpet, which is famous for producing many notable personalities from business, sports, and cinema. After completing his schooling, he joined St. Mary’s College, Hyderabad for his undergraduate studies. Although he did not complete a traditional professional degree because of his early entry into films, his time in college helped him build lifelong friendships and develop an independent personality.
  • During his school and college days, Ram Charan was more inclined toward sports and physical activities. He excelled particularly in horse riding, a passion that began in childhood and continues today—he even owns a polo team. He also showed interest in martial arts, which later became very helpful for performing his own stunts in films.

Family

  • His father, Chiranjeevi, is one of the most celebrated actors in Indian cinema history, often referred to as the “Megastar” of Telugu films. His mother, Surekha, is the daughter of legendary Telugu comic actor Allu Ramalingaiah, making Ram Charan a part of two of the most influential film families in South India — the Konidela and Allu families.
  • Ram Charan grew up surrounded by this rich film heritage. His uncles include actor-politician Pawan Kalyan and producer-actor Nagendra Babu, both of whom are major figures in Telugu cinema. On his mother’s side, his cousins include popular stars Allu Arjun, known for his stylish dance and mass films, and Allu Sirish, as well as producer Allu Aravind, who owns Geetha Arts, one of Telugu cinema’s biggest production houses.
  • Despite coming from a superstar family, Ram Charan often says his upbringing was quite disciplined. His parents ensured he and his sisters — Sushmitha and Srija — were brought up with strong values and understood the importance of hard work. Chiranjeevi, despite his busy career, is known to have been a strict but loving father, focusing on their studies and moral upbringing.
  • Family traditions, spirituality, and cultural practices played a huge role in shaping Ram Charan. Even as a child, he accompanied his parents on visits to temples and festivals. He grew up witnessing large family gatherings during Sankranti, Ugadi, and other Telugu festivals, which helped him remain close to his roots despite the film.
